# Mighty Mouse Variant Test Order

## First Wave
Use these three variants first:

### 1. Variant A, Compact Core
Why first:
- lowest friction
- strong baseline for usability
- likely closest to what people will tolerate as a slash command in normal work

### 2. Variant B, Structured Full
Why second:
- strongest explicit discipline
- likely best chance to reduce drift and fake confidence
- useful contrast against Variant A

### 3. Variant C, Scope-First
Why third:
- specifically targets one of the biggest small-model problems: drift
- likely to outperform on constrained edits and trap tasks

## Second Wave
Test after the first three establish signal:

### 4. Variant D, Honesty-First
Use when false-success and fake verification remain too high.

### 5. Variant E, Repair-Aware
Use once retry behavior becomes a meaningful differentiator.

## Decision Logic
After the first wave:
- keep the best performer as current leader
- mutate the winner
- only bring in second-wave variants when the scorecard shows a clear need

## Immediate Recommendation
Start with:
- baseline, no Mighty Mouse
- Variant A
- Variant B
- Variant C

That is the cleanest first comparison set.
